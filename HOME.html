KOLKATA STREET FOOD: 15 MOUTH WATERING STREET FOODS IN KOLKATA

Here's a list of Kolkata Street Food that one has to enjoy when in Kolkata:-

  1. Puchkas

    Puchkas is the most easily available street food of Kolkata. This small packs of crunchy balls filled with aloo sabji and tangy tamarind water is just what a foodie likes as an evening snack.The best puchkas in kolkata are available at Viveknanda Park, and Dakshinapan Shopping Complex Yard .

  2. Kathi Rolls

    Did you know the famous 'Kathi Rolls' were first invented in Kolkata? This shop called Nizam's owns the credit to the delicious stuffing of meat within in paratha that is now found in every city. Though there have been variations and twists as it found its way across cities, the original and authentic Kathi Rolls can still be found at Badshah. For more lip smacking? rolls, try out Aminia in New Market and Kusum Roll's Park Street!

  3. Dacre's Lane A.K.A Street Food Pradise

    Dacre's Lane offers a foodie everything that he can long for. This area is blessed with all kinds street foods from Chinese to Indian breakfast at Chitto Babus Shop, from beverages to lunch meal, from spicy "bhajabhuji's" at Apanjon to mood freshing lassi.

  4. Anadi Cabin, Esplanade Crossing

    In this time of 2 minutes instant noodles if you have a little more time then you can try out India's authentic Mughlai snacks at Anadi Cabin. This shop continues to mantain the great taste.

  5. Lord's More, Lake Gardens

    Right opposite to South City Mall, are a slew of eateries that will take your breath away. Whether you talk about the local phuchkas, chaat, momos, sweet and savory shops, bakeries, or shacks selling Chinese food, there's nothing to not be loved. Watch out for a certain Dragon Chicken from the smaller establishments while you're on this stretch.

  6. Rabindra Sadan Metro Exit

    The Tibetan cuisine has always been a favourite in Kolkata, and Rabindra Sadan Metro Exit is one of the best places to have these amazing dumplings. Momos here are served with a piping hot soup and the perfect sauce for accompaniments- the very definition of comfort food! A line of little establishments dot this area and they are always flocked by students and office goers in large numbers. The momos here are breathtaking, and the low prices will leave you amazed and craving for more!

  7. Ganguram's

    If you're in Kolkata, your Kolkata Street Food experience will be incomplete if you don'tt have Kochuri! The Kochuri or Radhabollobi, is one of its kind at Ganguram's. To finish, simply wash it down with some amazing Mishti Doi, or treat yourself to their sweet nothings!

  8. Kalika Mukhorochok Telebhaja, College Street

    If you are thinking to have something "chatpata" in the evening, go and grab "telabhaja" from Kalika.Whether you are planning for a solo evening or hanging out with friends in the streets of Kolkata, the telebhaja from Kalika will enhance the party mood.

  9. Balwant Singh Eating House, S.P Mukherjee Road

    Kolkata's culture of Tea can be aptly observed by just one visit to this historical place. While their tea, which comes in different 'bhaanr' (earthen cup) sizes is the absolute best, this isn't the only reason why Balwant Singh Eating House is so well loved. A unique offering called the doodh cola is available here, which is cola married to milk in a most joyous union. One of a kind! To peril your hunger you can also go for kachoori-sabji along with jalebi.

  10. Mayaram's, Lord Sinha Road: Paaavvvv Bhaajjiiiii? Slurpp!

    If you have a pav bhaji craving while you're in Kolkata, this is where you should be heading to! Mayaram's makes one of the best pav bhajis in all of Kolkata. If you're crossing the shop on your way, I assure you, you can't go on without surrendering to the charms of a plate of the most fragrant, paradisaical pav bhaji! Note: While you're on Lord Sinha Road, try the gola, or chuski, as it is more fondly known and the soda shikanji. They are served in sizeable earthen glasses and are absolutely great on a Kolkata afternoon, before and after those shopping sprees.

  11. Mitra Caf√©: For the best non-veg street food in Kolkata!

    Cabin culture in Kolkata was first introduced by Mitra Cafe. Your evening can be more delightful if it consists of fishfry, fishkobiraji, muttoncutlet and delicious pudding from MItra Cafe.

  12. Zakaria Street

    The paradise for Kebab lovers is what is you call Zakaria Street. This place offers variety of chicken and beef kebabs at Adam's Kebab Shop and varities of sweet di shes along with delicious "haleem".This place gets enriched with other kinds of snacks, fried items during Ramdaan.

  13. Greater Kolkata Salt Lake

    Salt Lake is the IT hub of our state. To encounter the hunger of all the people working there NewTown Food Hub is always ready with instant snacks and meals.

  14. Chilla, Daal Pakori, Vardaan Market

    Vardaan Market, being at the heart of the business district of Camac Street, is home to some of the best vegetarian delicacies that you can find in Kolkata. Try phuchkas from vendors near Westside and Pantaloons and Marwari delicacies like the Chilla, Daal pakori etc. at Vardaan Market!

  15. Ghoti Gorom, Jhalmuri in the Ganga Ghats

    If you are on a date with your crush or just vibing with your friends or alone at the Princep Ghats , the best partner with your conversation wil be the iconic Ghoti Gorom and Jhalmuri. It has the taste to make "you two" fall in love with both the snack and each other, just try it out.
